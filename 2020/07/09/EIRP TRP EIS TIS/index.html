EIRP与TRP

note: 部分内容用英语描述较方便，故本页会中英混合. 简单起见，且文中不考虑各种损耗.

EIRP：Effective(Equivalent) Isotropic Radiated Power.
(1)In antenna measurements, the measured radiated power in a single direction (that is, for a fixed $\theta$ and $\phi$) is known as the EIRP.
(2)EIRP can also be thought of as the amount of power a perfectly isotropic antenna would need to radiate to achieve the measured value.

这里再给出Antenna Design For Mobile Devices[1, p.254]中的解释.
Shown in Figure. 1 is an illustration of EIRP. Assume there is a 0 dBm signal radiated from an isotropic antenna and it travels outward in an ideal free space. Obviously, the isotropic assumption is a theoretical one, as there is no such antenna in the real world. At a given distance R, the theoretical received signal from the isotropic antenna is X dBm. Now replace the isotropic antenna with a phone, if the power of the received signal increases by 3 dB, the EIRP of the phone at that angular direction is 3dB above 0dBm, which equals 3dBm. The EIRP is a relative measurement, so no matter what distance R is, the EIRP of a phone is always the same.

note: 上面是Antenna Theory和Antenna Design给出的定义. Antenna Theory*的第一种定义感觉有问题，因为总的发射功率是一定的，阵列的方向性增益并不会凭空提供更多功率，增加的是功率密度. 所以，在某个方向上辐射的功率这种说法存疑.由[1]中定义也可知，实测中也不可能出现EIRP大于发射功率的情况，只是相对于一个理想的isotropic antenna的理想值去对比，然后定义这样一个量来形容待测天线/设备的辐射能力或这说方向性.(这里istropic的XdB功率应该是仿真数值，毕竟实际并不存在完美的isotropic天线)

再结合Wiki上的定义，
Wiki：Effective isotropic radiated power measures the amount of power a radio transmitter and antenna (or other source of electromagnetic waves) radiates in a specific direction: in the direction of maximum signal strength (the “main lobe”) of its radiation pattern. (应该也可以是等效任意方向，不一定是最大功率密度方向).

This apparent power is dependent on two factors: the total power output and the radiation pattern of the antenna – how much of that power is radiated in the desired direction. The latter factor is quantified by the antenna gain, which is the ratio of the signal strength radiated by an antenna to that radiated by a standard antenna.

For example, a 1,000-watt transmitter feeding an antenna with a gain of 4 (6 dBi) will have the same signal strength in the direction of its main lobe, and thus the same EIRP, as a 4,000-watt transmitter feeding an antenna with a gain of 1 (0 dBi). So EIRP is measure of radiated power that can compare different combinations of transmitters and antennas on an equal basis.

In spite of the names, EIRP does not measure transmitter power, or total power radiated by the antenna, they are just a measure of signal strength along the main lobe. They give no information about power radiated in other directions, or total power. EIRP is always greater than the actual total power radiated by the antenna.

简单来说就是，EIRP是在特定方向上的输出功率，不是总功率，是对功率密度的一种表征. 我认为不存在“在某个方向上辐射的功率”这种说法，有的是功率密度，功率量还要考虑接收的有效面积. 只能说这是一种定义上的表示，用来快速对两个不同的天线的效能进行对比，或者体现该天线或任意发射机的效能和方向性.

EIS：Effective Isotropic Sensitivity
In antenna measurements, the measured sensitivity in a single direction (that is, for a fixed $\theta$ and $\phi$) is known as the EIS.

TIS：Total Isotropic Sensitivity
TIS, is a measure of the average sensitivity of a receiver-antenna system, when averaged over the entire 3-dimensional sphere.
